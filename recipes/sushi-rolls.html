Sushi Rolls

Description

Making sushi at home can be be a fun experience for you and your guests and can help save you money if you like to eat a lot of it. Sushi at its core can be customized to your liking. Feel free to add any kind of fish or veggies which you enjoy. Just be mindful not to overstuff your rolls or they will come apart.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/3 cups water
  • 2/3 cup uncooked short-grain white rice
  • 3 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 sheets nori seaweed
  • 1/2 pound imitation crabmeat, flaked
  • 1 avocado-peeled, pitted, and sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber, peeled, cut into small strips
  • 2 tablespoons pickled ginger

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F
  2. Boil water in a medium pot stir in rice. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and water absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
  3. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a bowl. Stir mixture into cooked rice.
  4. Lay nori sheets on a baking sheet. Heat nori in oven until warm, 1 to 2 minutes
  5. Center warm nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat. Use wet hands to spread a thin layer of rice on top.
  6. Arrange 1/4 of the crabmeat, avocado, cucumber, and pickled ginger over the rice in a line down the middle.
  7. Lift one end of the mat and roll tightly over the filling.
  8. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  9. Use a wet, sharp knife to cut each roll into 4 to 6 slices.
